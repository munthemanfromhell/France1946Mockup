# Research notes

Core historical points represented in the mockup:

- The election of 10 November 1946 chose the first National Assembly of the Fourth Republic.
- PCF: 182 seats; MRP: 173; SFIO: 102; RGR: 69; Moderates: 72; remaining groups: 29.
- The SFIO had fallen from 128 seats in the June constituent election.
- Guy Mollet defeated the Daniel Mayer leadership at the SFIO congress and became secretary-general on 4 September 1946.
- Maurice Thorez sought the headship of government but lacked a majority; part of the Socialist group refused to support him.
- Léon Blum ultimately formed a homogeneous Socialist provisional government in December 1946 after coalition negotiations failed.
- Paul Ramadier later led the broad government formed when the permanent institutions of the Fourth Republic were installed.
